What we measure

Metrics & trend

Accuracy, win-rate, an exact/parent rubric, fast-path segmented out, coverage always shown.

Requirements

  1. The owner sees how often the new categorizer was right, and how that compares to the current one.
  2. Easy-by-construction cases never inflate the headline accuracy number.
  3. Recent runs are marked provisional until enough time has passed for the owner to have reviewed them.
  4. When a number moves, the owner can tell whether a categorizer change or just the data caused it.

The numbers

  • % corrected = agent decisions you later recategorized / agent decisions in the cohort. This is the primary signal — the act of correcting a wrong row is the label, no confirm step needed.
  • Agent % correct = 1 − % corrected, over rows past the settling window (uncorrected = correct, because you reviewed the emailed report).
  • Legacy % correct = where your final key equals legacy_key (does the correction land back on what legacy chose?).
  • Agent-vs-legacy win-rate = rows where your final key equals the agent's but not legacy's, vs the reverse.

Grading rubric

Graded against your final taxonomy key (the category after any correction you made):

  • Correct — exact key match.
  • Partial — right parent, wrong child.
  • Incorrect — wrong parent.

Track exact-match and parent-level accuracy separately — a wrong child matters far less than a wrong parent, and conflating them hides real movement.

Segmentation & honesty

  • Exclude fast-path from the accuracy denominator — it is verified-reuse and ~100% by construction. Use the stored method_at_eval_time flag, never a live recompute (verification state drifts day to day).
  • Settling window. A row counts as "correct" only after enough time has passed for you to have reviewed its report (a tunable window, default ~7 days). Newer runs are provisional. This is the cost of having no explicit confirm step: "uncorrected" can't be distinguished from "not-yet-reviewed" until the window elapses — mitigated by emailing every disagreement run so you actually see it.
  • Corrections do not set is_verified, so a correction does not silently become a fast-path hit on later days — the feedback loop is damped (see assumptions).

The trend page

A static HTML page rendered from the eval store, computed lazily on render (no submission-time trigger). Eval runs fire twice daily (every 12h) and points aggregate by day, with version-stamp annotations on the x-axis where any stamp field changes. This is the artifact that answers "is it getting better, and because of what?"
