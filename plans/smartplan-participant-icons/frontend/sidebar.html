History sidebar

Feature 1: shared conversations in ChatHistoryDrawer get two overlapping member avatars on the right edge of the entry.

Requirements

  1. Scanning the drawer, shared conversations are identifiable in under a second by the pair of faces on the right.
  2. Personal conversations look exactly as they do today.
  3. Long titles never collide with the avatars — the title truncates, the avatars hold their ground.

Entry layout

Each entry (frontend/src/ChatHistoryDrawer.tsx:115–129) is currently a <button class="block w-full truncate …"> holding only the title. Restructure to a flex row:

<button class="flex w-full items-center gap-2 …">
  <span class="min-w-0 flex-1 truncate">{title}</span>
  {isMultiUser && <AvatarStack people={stackPeople} size="xs" />}
</button>
  • min-w-0 flex-1 truncate keeps today's ellipsis behaviour while ceding fixed space to the stack (shrink-0 on the stack side).
  • The drawer is a fixed 18rem (w-72) panel; two 20px discs with an 8px overlap cost ~32px — comfortably within budget.
  • Existing hover/selected styles (bg-cream-soft etc.) are untouched; the ring-paper on the discs matches the drawer's background family so overlap stays legible on hover.

The drawer receives members as a prop from AppShell (it already receives getToken), and its local Conversation interface gains session_mode.

When the stack shows, and who is in it

Show the stack when conversation.session_mode === "joint" && members.length >= 2 (the rule agreed during planning — see Conversations API for the alternatives considered).

Who appears: the first two household members in a stable order — the other member in front, the viewer tucked behind. In a two-person household that is simply "both of you", with the partner most visible, which matches what the indicator is telling you: they are in here too.

Degraded states

  • Members not yet loaded / fetch failed: members.length < 2, so no stack renders — the drawer is never blocked on the members call, and entries simply look like today until data arrives.
  • No profile picture: the Avatar initial fallback appears in the stack; a pair of initial-discs still reads as "shared".
  • Solo household with a joint conversation: possible (joint mode exists before an invite is accepted); the two-member condition keeps the stack away until the partner actually exists.
