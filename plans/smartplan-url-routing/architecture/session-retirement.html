Retiring the session module

The local-storage session mechanism was the pre-routing answer to "which conversation is open?". The URL now answers that, so the module and its key are deleted, not wrapped.

Requirements

  1. Opening a conversation from the history drawer is instant — no page reload, no flash of blank screen.
  2. The drawer always highlights the conversation the user is actually looking at.
  3. There is exactly one place that records which conversation is open, so the app can never disagree with itself about it.

What goes

frontend/src/session.ts is deleted in full. Its three exports and their call sites:

ExportUsed byFate
loadOrCreateSessionId()ChatScreen (mount-time useMemo)Replaced by the route param / minted-per-mount id (keying rule).
openConversation(id)ChatHistoryDrawer rowsReplaced by client-side links to /c/<id>.
startNewChat()ChatHistoryDrawer "New chat" buttonReplaced by a client-side link to /.
SESSION_KEY (penny:sessionId)Drawer's active-row highlightHighlight derives from the route param instead. The stored key is never read again (legacy note).

With it goes the window.location.assign("/") full-reload pattern — the last non-router navigation in the app.

What replaces it

Nothing, deliberately — no new module. "Which conversation is open" becomes a fact about the URL, read where needed:

  • The route table passes the :id param into the chat screen.
  • The drawer reads the same param (via useParams() or the location) for its highlight.
  • Navigation is the router's <Link> / useNavigate() — no bespoke helpers wrapping them, per the "distrust shallow pass-throughs" design rule. If a wrapper ever earns its keep (e.g. a typed conversationPath(id) builder used from 3+ places), it can be added when the third caller exists.

Drawer rows become links

Each drawer row (currently a <button onClick=...>) becomes a <Link to={"/c/" + id}> styled identically, and "New chat" becomes <Link to="/">. Real links buy middle-click / cmd-click "open in new tab" for free — one of the quiet payoffs of routing. Two behaviors to preserve:

  • Mobile drawer dismissal — on the overlay (mobile) layout, navigating should also close the drawer (call onClose in the link's click handler; the navigation itself is left to the link).
  • aria-current — moves from the local-storage comparison to the route param comparison; the active row keeps its highlight through client-side switches without a refetch.

The drawer's list-refetch-on-open behavior is unchanged; a fresh conversation still appears in the list the next time the drawer opens, once the backend has persisted and titled it.
